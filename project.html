City Design Project

About the Project

Our City Design project is a bold vision for the future, focusing on creating an eco-friendly, resource-efficient, and socially inclusive urban environment. By integrating innovative strategies, we aim to enhance energy efficiency, reduce waste, and improve transportation, all while promoting community well-being. This project addresses critical urban challenges such as overpopulation, resource depletion, and environmental sustainability. With a holistic approach, we strive to build a city that thrives on green solutions and inclusivity, ensuring a better quality of life for all its residents.

Phase 1: City Map & Topography

Amarapalli, near Hyderabad in Telangana (Pincode: 500001), spans 1500 sq km on the Deccan Plateau, characterized by rugged terrain, small hills, and lateritic soils. It has a tropical climate with hot summers, moderate rainfall, and mild winters. The city features modern infrastructure, traditional architecture, and strong connectivity via roads and rail. Its economy is shaped by proximity to IT hubs, industries, and agriculture. Rich in cultural heritage influenced by Qutb Shahi and Asaf Jahi dynasties, the city predominantly speaks Telugu and Urdu. Biodiversity includes scrub forests, thorny bushes, and semi-arid wildlife.
